Co-founder and Organiser

I co-founded Hack Keele society at Keele University and help to run it. I wanted to create a collaborative community within the university for proactive students interested in technology to share knowledge and work on projects. I created all our branding and the website, as well as helping to manage the social media.


We hold meetups every week, where we work on projects and teach relevant technical skills for the workplace, including how to use GitHub and Docker. Additionally, we have a Hack Day planned and a 24 hour MLH hackathon set for next year. I help to fundraise and personally manage all connections to external organisations.

HM Government

Cyber Internship

During my internship at a Government department I undertook an intense cyber training course aimed at transforming students into highly competent ‘Cyberists’. A wide range of skills were taught which included, evasion & ethical pen testing, secure software engineering, networking, reverse engineering, low level OS & windows internals, C programming, information assurance and IOT.


I also worked in teams for CTF’s, packet analysis, SOC and IA challenges. Adding to this I undertook a personal project. I decided to create a cyber security chatbot that would help make naïve users more secure by providing a more intuitive way to interact with cyber security whilst abstracting the complexities.
